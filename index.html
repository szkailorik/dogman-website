Origin Story

Dog Man is an American children's graphic novel series by author and illustrator Dav Pilkey. A spinoff of the Captain Underpants series, Dog Man follows the adventures of a half-man, half-dog hybrid superhero who works as a police officer.

The story originated within the pages of Captain Underpants, where protagonists George Beard and Harold Hutchins created Dog Man in their notebooks. In Dog Man's world, Officer Knight and his K9 partner Greg were injured in an explosion orchestrated by villain cat Petey. At the hospital, doctors couldn't save both, but a nurse suggested sewing Greg's head onto Knight's body. Thus, Dog Man was born!

Dog Man has Knight's crime-fighting abilities but Greg's personalityâ€”he loves licking people, chasing balls, and is afraid of vacuum cleaners. Though he can't speak, he's the city's most reliable guardian.

Quick Facts

  • Full Name: David Murray Pilkey Jr.
  • Born: March 4, 1966
  • Birthplace: Cleveland, Ohio, USA
  • Profession: Author, Illustrator
  • Notable Works: Captain Underpants, Dog Man, Ricky Ricotta

Origin of the Pen Name

Dav Pilkey's pen name originated from a funny accident. While working at Pizza Hut, his nametag accidentally read "Dav" instead of "Dave," and he adopted this unique spelling ever since.

Overcoming Challenges

Pilkey suffered from attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and dyslexia, which caused him frequent difficulties at school. He was often sent to sit in the hallway for being "disruptive" in class. But it was in that very hallway where young Pilkey began creating his own stories and comics. Both Captain Underpants and Dog Man were born in that hallway!

Career

In 1984, Pilkey began attending Kent State University as an art major. During his freshman year, an English professor suggested he write a children's book. He created "World War Won" and won first place in the National Written and Illustrated by Awards Contest for Students, published in 1987. He has since created many beloved series.

An Inspirational Story

Pilkey's story teaches us: What seems like a weakness can sometimes become a unique strength. It was precisely because he was "misbehaving" in class that we have these imaginative and humorous works today. His success has inspired countless children with learning disabilitiesâ€”what makes you different might be your greatest gift.
